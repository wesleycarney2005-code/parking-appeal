Common questions

Does this actually work?

Over 40% of UK parking penalty charge appeals succeed at the first stage — and many more succeed on appeal to an independent adjudicator. A well-written letter referencing the correct legislation dramatically improves your chances.

What types of fines does it cover?

Council penalty charge notices (PCNs), private parking charge notices, bus lane fines, CCTV parking fines, and moving traffic contraventions.

How long do I have to appeal?

For council fines, you typically have 28 days to appeal (or 14 days to pay at the discounted rate). Private parking notices usually allow 28 days. Don't delay — generate your letter today.

Do I need to pay even if I appeal?

No. You do not need to pay the fine while your appeal is being considered. Once you submit a formal appeal, enforcement is suspended until a decision is made.

What if my appeal is rejected?

You have the right to take your case to an independent adjudicator (POPLA for private fines, or the Traffic Penalty Tribunal for council fines). Our letter includes guidance on this process.
